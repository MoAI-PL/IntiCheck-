Discover IntiCheck

Bridging the gap between "Dr. Google" panic and a professional clinical diagnosis.

Why are we doing this?

Shame and fear are health's greatest enemies. When a concerning lesion appears on our skin—especially in intimate areas—we often delay visiting a doctor. Instead, we search for images online, which usually leads to unnecessary anxiety.

We created IntiCheck to provide a discreet, non-judgmental space. Our goal is not to replace a physician, but to reduce your stress and motivate you to take the correct medical steps.

What exactly do we do?

IntiCheck is a digital medical assistant serving as a Triage system (preliminary screening). When you upload a photo of a skin concern, our system rapidly analyzes and classifies it into one of the primary categories (e.g., viral infections, fungal issues, nail pathologies, or suspicious pigmented lesions).

Instead of throwing complex medical jargon at you, the app explains in simple terms: "What you might be dealing with, what immediate actions to take (to avoid worsening the condition), and exactly which specialist you should consult."

How does it work under the hood?

The heart of the application is a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) based on the lightweight, optimized MobileNetV2 architecture. The model doesn't rely on simple guessing—it processes the uploaded image as standardized tensors (224x224 px), subjecting them to mathematical analysis to detect unique patterns across 6 different classes of conditions.

To ensure maximum reliability, we applied hard data augmentation during training, and addressed the common issue of medical data imbalance algorithmically using Class Weights.

Our backend architecture guarantees complete anonymity. Your photos never touch the server's hard drive. We utilize In-Memory Processing—the file is analyzed exclusively in the volatile RAM as a byte stream. Within a fraction of a second after generating the prediction, the image matrix is immediately and permanently destroyed.

Important Note:
The current version of the algorithm is in a prototype phase. While the system can effectively recognize core patterns, it may produce errors under unusual lighting or for rare conditions. The AI result is a suggestion only and never replaces professional medical advice.
